Theme of the Sunday: Not the One Who Speaks. . . But the One Who Acts.     The gospel of today tells us clearly how life based on words and not on deeds of love, will not withstand the violent storm of God’s judgement. The first reading is linked to the gospel because in it God recommends the practice of the law, not just words. The second reading tells us that “justification” is a free gift of God and that to reach salvation we need such justification to be manifested through our good works.

Year B

Theme of the Sunday: A Person Is Not a Slave of Any Law.    The readings today are all related through the theme of “Sabbath.” The first describes God’s decree to rest on Saturday. Even animals are to rest. This law was enacted to protect the poor. The gospel shows us how Jesus interpreted the observance of the Sabbath. By stating that “the Sabbath is for man” he declared that not only the Sabbath, but every law is subordinated to the good of people. The second reading is not on the Sabbath but can be linked to it because Paul is stressing that God chooses the poor and the little. Was it not to defend them that God gave us the law of rest?

Year C

Theme of the Sunday: What? Are the Pagans Better Than Us?    The gospel tells us to look with respect and sympathy on those whose culture, way of thinking, and religion differ from us. The Spirit is at work in them, too. They are often more docile to his inspiration than we are. The first reading prepares our hearts to open up to those who do not belong to our group. It says that the temple of Lord will be opened to all people. Christianity is a universal religion, but from its very beginnings there were those who tried to lead it back into the narrow confines of one people. The second reading tells us that this was tried, for instance, in Galatia. It did not succeed. It would have subverted the Gospel of Christ.
