APRIL 4 St Isidore, Bishop and Doctor of the Church or St Benedict

(560-636). He was born in Seville, Spain. He was Archbishop of Seville for thirty-five years and was well known in the fourth Council of Toledo (633) for his leadership. He strengthened Catholicism in Spain; he was admired for his preaching, his writings, and his miracles. His most extensive work (Etymologies) was an encyclopedia which continued to be used throughout the Middle Ages.

Common of Pastors (for bishops) or of Doctors of the Church

Introductory Rites

COLLECT

Graciously hear the prayers, O Lord, which we make in commemoration of Saint Isidore, that your Church may be aided by his intercession, just as she has been instructed by his heavenly teaching. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.

St Benedict, Religious

Common of Religious

Introductory Rites

COLLECT

O God, who choose the weak and the simple to reveal your power and greatness; grant us the grace to follow the example of Saint Benedict in his spirit of service and prayer, so that we too, like him, may be chosen instruments in the building of your Kingdom. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
