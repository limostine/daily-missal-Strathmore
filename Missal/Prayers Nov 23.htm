NOVEMBER 23 St Clement I, Pope and Martyr (or St Columban)

(+97). He was the third pope after St Peter. He wrote the famous epistle to the Corinthians, commanding them to seek peace and unity. We see in it the Providence of God who gives us a clear example of the Pope's universal jurisdiction, even before the year 100.

Common of martyrs or of pastors (for popes)

Introductory Rites

COLLECT

Almighty ever-living God, who are wonderful in the virtue of all your Saints, grant us joy in the yearly commemoration of Saint Clement, who, as a Martyr and High Priest of your Son, bore out by his witness what he celebrated in mystery and confirmed by example what he preached with his lips. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.

St Columban, abbot

(+615). An Irish monk, he went to France and founded many monasteries which he ruled with strict discipline.

Common of Pastors (for missionaries) or of holy men & women (for an Abbot)

Introductory Rites

COLLECT

O God, who in Saint Columban wonderfully joined the work of evangelisation to zeal for the monastic life, grant, we pray, that through his intercession and example we may strive to seek you above all things and to bring increase to your faithful people. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
