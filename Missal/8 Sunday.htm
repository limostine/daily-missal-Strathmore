Theme of the Sunday: God Is Father and Mother.     The first reading and the gospel complement each other. In the first reading God is compared to a mother, while in the gospel he is compared to a father who provides all that his children need. Can we be so foolish as to abandon God who is so good, to worship idols? Can money give us more comfort? At what cost? The second reading is also linked to this theme, because it tells us that Paul is not worried about what people say or think of him. He places all his trust in God.

Year B

Theme of the Sunday: Jesus the “Bridegroom.”    The first reading tells us that God is the faithful bridegroom, even if the people (the bride) betray him. The gospel expands on the theme of wedding. This time it is Jesus who is the bridegroom and who chooses the church as his bride. He does not love it because it is good. He accepts it as it is and by his love transforms it into a faithful bride. If the present time is wedding time, then our fasting will have a new meaning. The second reading brings to our attention the example of Paul, a man who responded to the love of Christ with generosity and loyalty.

Year C

Theme of the Sunday: Be Careful of So-called ‘Masters’!     The first reading gives us wise counsel. To ascertain what is in the heart of a person we should let them speak and we should listen carefully to what they say. The gospel gives us a series of “sayings” of Jesus about the “masters” of the Christian community. We should listen to what they say to see the store from which they draw their words, from the store of the Master or from the rubbish heap of their passions? The second reading is about our imperishable and immortal body. In the world to come we will have a new language, used only for praising the Lord. Here on earth we use it, at times, to cause harm to others.
