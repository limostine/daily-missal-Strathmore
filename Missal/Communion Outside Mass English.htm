COMMUNION OUTSIDE MASS

The priest unfolds the corporal, and facing the tabernacle says:

The Lord be with you.

R. And with your spirit.

Brethren (brothers and sisters), let us acknowledge our sins, and so prepare ourselves to celebrate the sacred mysteries.

Pause of silence.

I confess to almighty God and to you, my brothers and sisters, that I have greatly sinned, in my thoughts and in my words, in what I have done and in what I have failed to do, through my fault, through my fault, through my most grievous fault; therefore I ask blessed Mary ever-Virgin, all the Angels and Saints, and you, my brothers and sisters, to pray for me to the Lord our God.

The minister concludes:

May Almighty God have mercy on us, forgive us our sins, and bring us to everlasting life.

R. Amen.

The Priest could read a short scriptural text:

A reading from the holy gospel according to John (Io 6, 54-55).

Jesus says: “Anyone who does eat my flesh and drink my blood has eternal life, and I shall raise him up on the last day. For my flesh is real food and my blood is real drink.”

The Gospel of the Lord.

R. Praise to you, Lord Jesus Christ.

He opens the tabernacle, genuflects, places the ciborium on the corporals, and closes the tabernacle. Then he says:

At the Saviour’s command and formed by divine teaching, we dare to say:

He continues with the people:

Our Father…

Then he opens the ciborium, genuflects, takes a Host and turning towards the people, says:

Behold the Lamb of God, behold him who takes away the sins of the world. Blessed are those called to the supper of the Lamb.

R. Lord, I am not worthy that you should enter under my roof, but only say the word and my soul shall be healed.

He proceeds to distribute Communion as usual, saying:

The body of Christ

R. Amen.

He returns to the altar and purifies the tray and his fingers. Then he opens the tabernacle, leaves the ciborium inside, genuflects, and locks it. After folding the corporal he stands facing the tabernacle and manibus iunctis he says:

Let us pray.

Lord Jesus Christ, you gave us the Eucharist as the memorial of your suffering and death. May our worship of this sacrament of your body and blood help us to experience the salvation you won for us and the peace of the kingdom where you live with the Father and the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.

R. Amen.

During Ester Time the following prayer is preferred:

Let us pray.

Lord, you have nourished us with your Easter sacraments. Fill us with your Spirit and make us one in peace and love. We ask this through Christ our Lord.

R. Amen.

Turning towards the people, extending his hands as usual, he says:

The Lord be with you.

R. And with your spirit.

May almighty God bless you, the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

R. Amen.

Go in the peace of Christ.

R. Thanks be to God.
